📊 Tableau Dashboards Repository
🔎 Overview
This repository contains a collection of Tableau dashboards designed to analyze customer reviews, entertainment catalog data, and airline performance. Each dashboard leverages Tableau’s interactive features—filters, maps, trend lines, and drill‑downs—to transform raw datasets into strategic insights.

The dashboards demonstrate how Tableau can be used to visualize complex datasets, uncover hidden patterns, and support data‑driven decision making across industries.

📈 Dashboard Explanations
1. British Airways Review Dashboard
Purpose: Analyzes customer satisfaction across service dimensions, aircraft types, and regions.

Highlights:

Average Overall Rating: 4.2

Service breakdown: Cabin Staff (3.3), Entertainment (1.4), Food & Beverages (2.4), Ground Service (3.0), Seat Comfort (2.9), Value for Money (2.8).

Global map showing average ratings by country.

Trend analysis from 2017–2023, highlighting fluctuations in customer sentiment.

Aircraft‑wise ratings: Boeing 747‑400 highest at 4.7, A321 lowest at 3.6.

Review volumes per aircraft ensure statistically valid comparisons.

Value: Enables airline management to benchmark fleet performance, identify weak service areas, and track customer sentiment globally.

2. Netflix Content Analysis Dashboard
Purpose: Provides insights into Netflix’s catalog composition, ratings, and genre distribution.

Highlights:

Content distribution: Movies (68.42%) vs TV Shows (31.58%).

Growth trend: Significant increase in titles after 2015, peaking around 2020.

Ratings breakdown: TV‑MA (2,027), TV‑14 (1,698), TV‑PG (701), R (508), PG‑13 (286), and others.

Global map showing movie distribution by country, with 488 titles marked “unknown.”

Top 10 genres include Documentaries, Stand‑Up Comedy, Dramas, Independent Movies, Kids’ TV, and International Movies.

Title‑level detail (e.g., 1st Summoning, 2018, Horror/Independent, 94 minutes, TV‑MA).

Value: Helps content strategists understand catalog diversity, regional distribution, and genre popularity for better programming and licensing decisions.
